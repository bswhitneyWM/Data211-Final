Country Breakdown of Movie Ratings

The final element I investigated was the country's average score for a movie. Below is a Tableau dashboard consisting of a choropleth showing a movie's average voter score when produced in each country, and a filterable bar graph ranking the countries with the highest average score. The interactive dashboard can be found here.

The Tableau dashboard showing each country's average movie score. Valid scores fall in the range between 0 and 10. Only countries that produced at least 5 movies were included in the bar graph.

The average movie ratings between countries are significantly more clumped together than the number of movies produced and average revenue are. Turkey, with an average score of 7.257, deserves recognition for being the only country with an average score over 7, but the difference between Israel in the second spot and Norway in the tenth spot is only .33. In fact, no country with at least 5 movies drops below an average rating of 5.4. From the choropleth map we see a lot of countries in South America and Africa have very high average ratings, but since most of these countries don't show up on the bar chart, it suggests these high ratings may be due to producing one or two good movies and nothing else, which is corroborated by the histogram in the tool tip.
