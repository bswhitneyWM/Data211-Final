Introduction

The popularity of movies stems form the fact they let people escape reality and fully engage in a new, interesting story of their choice. However, it is very difficult to predict whether a movie will be good or bad before watching it. A movie's success is often measured by how much money it brings in or the ratings it receives from viewers, but none of this information is known until far after the movie is released. The goal of this project is to investigate what movie factors are strongly correlated with a movie's revenue, and to identify any patterns that emerge from a country-level analysis of these factors.

The dataset I used originates from Kaggle and includes information on over 48,000 movies including the title, genre, budget, revenue brought in, average review score and number of votes, production country, and much more. All movies included in the dataset were released before July 2017.
