MOVIE ANALYSIS

Bryce Whitney

Factors Associated with Revenue

From the start I was interested in exploring how different components and factors in movies are correlated with a movie's revenue, as revenue is often used as a metric to determine the success or failure of a movie. Specifically, I decided to explore a movie's budget, runtime, and average voter score were correlated with a movie's revenue. Movies with missing data in any of those 4 fields were not included, leaving 5360 movies to be analyzed.

A heatmap showing the correlation coefficient between each variable and revenue, and a corresponding hexbin plot for each variable.

From the above figure, we see that budget has the strongest linear correlation with revenue, boasting a correlation coefficient of 0.73. This isn't too surprising because I would expect movies with higher budgets to have better actors and actresses, better special effects, better advertising, and simply allow for a better product overall. Contrarily, a movie's runtime and average score have very weak correlations with revenue. This is corroborated by the hexbin plots where for average score and runtime loosely follow skewed normal distributions, yet there is a clear positive linear trend in the budget hexbin plot. While causation can't be extracted from the data, it does highlight that budget could be one of the most important factors for predicting how much money a movie will bring in.
